Who we are

NHS Digital provides national information, data and IT systems for health and care services.

Mentions of "us" and "we" mean NHS Digital and "you" means anyone using the platform.

We've created a website that lets you understand what NHS login is.

We need personal information from you if you want to onboard with NHS login.

A data controller determines how and why personal data is processed. We're the data controller for your personal information you give us when:

  • you contact us

We'll use your personal information in line with data protection laws and this privacy notice.

What personal information we collect about you and why

We only collect personal information from you:

  • to verify who you are
  • to provide you with onboarding material and process you through NHS login onboarding
  • to improve our service

The legal basis for using your personal information

We'll only collect, use and share your personal information when we've an appropriate legal basis to do so.

For the purposes of the health websites and apps, our use of your personal information is necessary to comply with a legal obligation we have.

Read more about our legal position.

How we share your personal information

We sometimes use other organisations to process your personal information on our behalf. When we do, these organisations are bound by our legal agreements to ensure your personal information is secure and used only for the purpose we stipulate.

We may need to share your personal information if we are required to do so by law.

How we protect your personal information and keep it secure

We take the security of your personal information seriously. We've set up security measures, policies and procedures such as:

  • we train all staff in data and security protection
  • monitor our platform to keep your personal information secure
  • use two-factor authentication
  • follow good practice guidance provided by the National Technical Authority
  • we use legally binding agreements with all organisations we use
  • our staff agree to security and confidentiality policies before they're given access to personal information for training purposes

How long we hold onto your personal information

We hold onto (retain) your personal information for as long as is reasonably necessary. In some circumstances the length of time we hold onto your personal information will depend on legal, regulatory, tax, accounting, or necessary technical requirements.

In any event, we retain your records in line with the Records Management Code of Practice for Health and Social Care (2016).

Where your data is stored

We store your data in the European Economic Area (EEA). We'll update this notice if we transfer your personal information outside of the EEA. If this happens, we'll make sure you are given the same level of protection.

Cookies

We put small files called cookies onto your device (for example your mobile phone).

We use:

  • analytic cookies to collect information about how you use our platform to help us make it better

Please read about our cookies for more information.

Your rights

Data protection laws give you rights in relation to your personal information.

Request a copy of your personal information:

If you would like to ask for a copy of your personal information, you need to complete a subject access request form.

You can either email or post it to the contact details on the form.

Correct your personal information errors or omissions:

You can request that we change your personal information if:

  • we've recorded your personal information incorrectly
  • your personal information is incomplete

We may check if the personal information we have is correct before changing it. We'll do this as quickly as possible and might ask you for more information.

Request your personal information is deleted:

You can request that we erase your personal information in limited circumstances where:

  • it's no longer needed for the purposes for which it was collected
  • you have withdrawn your consent (where the data processing was based on consent)
  • following a successful right to object (see right to object)
  • it's been processed unlawfully
  • to comply with a legal obligation to which we are subject

We may not be able to erase your personal information if the processing of your personal information is necessary:

  • for compliance with a legal obligation
  • for the establishment, exercise or defence of legal claims
  • to protect the rights of another natural or legal person

Request us to restrict our use of your personal information:

You have the right to request a restriction such as a temporary stop of the processing of your personal information where:

  • you think the personal information is inaccurate and it should not be used until it's corrected
  • we're using your personal information unlawfully and you want your personal information to be held by us but not processed whilst a complaint / investigation takes place
  • you require us to keep your personal information and not delete it while you make or defend a legal claim
  • you have objected to our use of your personal information and we do not have legitimate grounds to override your objection

Objections and complaints

You have the right to complain about how we process your personal information. You can do this using our feedback and complaints page or through the Information Complaints Office (ICO). The ICO is the regulator for data protection.

Changes to our terms of use

Our terms of use may change. We'll inform you on the platform if we make any substantial changes NHS login's privacy notice, cookies or terms and conditions.
